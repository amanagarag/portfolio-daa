Course Learning Reflections

Types of Problems in Nature

  • Iteration: Repeating a process a certain number of times. Example: Looping through a list of items.
  • Recursion: Breaking down a problem into smaller, self-similar subproblems. Example: Calculating factorial.
  • Backtracking: Trying different solutions and undoing them if they don't work. Example: Solving a maze.

Space and Time Efficiency

  • Space Efficiency: How much memory a program uses.
  • Time Efficiency: How fast a program runs.
  • These are important because they determine how well a program performs, especially for large datasets.
  • Different classes of problems have different growth rates (e.g., linear, logarithmic, exponential), which affect their efficiency.
